Interpolation polynomial in the Lagrange form and its first derivative

The second order Lagrange polynomial can be written as:

$$p(x) = \frac{(x-x_1)(x-x_2)}{(x_0-x_1)(x_0-x_2)}y_0 + \frac{(x-x_0)(x-x_2)}{(x_1-x_0)(x_1-x_2)}y_1 + \frac{(x-x_0)(x-x_1)}{(x_2-x_0)(x_2-x_1)}y_2$$

And its first derivative is:

$$p'(x) = \frac{(x - x_1) + (x - x_2)}{(x_0-x_1)(x_0-x_2)}y_0 + \frac{(x - x_0) + (x - x_2)}{(x_1-x_0)(x_1-x_2)}y_1 + \frac{(x - x_0) + (x - x_1)}{(x_2-x_0)(x_2-x_1)}y_2$$

If the nodes $x_0$, $x_1$ and $x_2$ are equispaced by a step $\Delta x$, then the calculated derivatives at the nodes can be expressed as:

$$p'(x_0) = \frac{-3y_0 + 4y_1 - y_2}{2\Delta x}$$ $$p'(x_1) = \frac{-y_0 + y_2}{2\Delta x}$$ $$p'(x_2) = \frac{y_0 - 4y_1 + 3y_2}{2\Delta x}$$

Notice that the expression for the derivative at $x_1$ is equivalent to the derivative calculated using the central differences method.
